About

A marketer shaped by film, culture, and global brand work.

I care about the part of marketing that people actually feel: the story, the tone, the trust, the taste, and the relationship between a brand and the people it wants to matter to.

I was born in the United States and grew up in San José, Costa Rica. I started out as a documentary filmmaker and photographer, later producing branded films in Atlanta and moving into brand and partnership work across major airline brands in New York.

My background is a little unusual on purpose. Film taught me how to shape meaning. Marketing taught me how to make that meaning travel.

My story

The throughline has always been storytelling.

I started by making documentaries, taking photos, and touring with DJs and bands. That period taught me how culture moves in real life, not just in decks.

Over time I became more interested in the strategic side of creative work: positioning, audience psychology, partnerships, messaging, and how a brand becomes memorable without becoming generic.

Today, I like work that asks for both sides of my brain: the operational side that can build a program, and the creative side that knows when something feels flat.

Timeline

A few places that shaped how I work.

My point of view comes from moving between places, formats, and disciplines. Each stop taught me a different piece of the work.

San José, Costa Rica

My storytelling roots. This is where I first started making things, paying attention to character, and falling in love with film and photography.

Atlanta

Branded storytelling, purpose films, and a deeper education in narrative strategy, production, and screenwriting at Emory University.

New York City

Global brand and partnership work, creator programs, and a closer view of how brands behave when culture and business move at the same speed.
