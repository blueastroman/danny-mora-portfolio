About

A marketer with a film background and a low tolerance for bland work.

I like marketing when it feels specific. The idea is clear, the tone is right, and the work doesn’t sound like it came from a deck no one believed in.

I was born in the United States and grew up in San José, Costa Rica. I started in documentary film and photography, later moved into branded storytelling in Atlanta, and eventually into brand and partnership work in New York.

Film taught me pacing, taste, and story. Marketing taught me how to use those things in the real world.

My story

The throughline has always been the same.

I started by making documentaries, taking photos, and touring with DJs and bands. It was a good education in how people actually respond to things in real life.

Over time I got more interested in the strategic side of creative work: positioning, audience, partnerships, messaging, and how to make a brand feel distinct without trying too hard.

Now I like work that uses both sides of my brain: the operational side that can build a program and the creative side that knows when something feels dead on arrival.

Timeline

A few places that shaped me.

My point of view comes from moving between places, formats, and disciplines. Each stop taught me a different piece of the work.

San José, Costa Rica

My storytelling roots. This is where I first started making things, paying attention to character, and falling in love with film and photography.

Atlanta

Branded storytelling, purpose films, and a deeper education in narrative strategy, production, and screenwriting at Emory University.

New York City

Global brand and partnership work, creator programs, and a closer view of how brands operate when everything moves fast.
