Notes

Things I keep thinking about.

A small space for thoughts on creator work, brand voice, attention, and how technology keeps changing the job.

These are working thoughts, not polished essays. I like keeping them here because they say as much about how I think as any case study does.

Spring 2026 / Trust

Trust is becoming the real growth lever.

A lot of marketing still acts like reach is the whole game. Reach matters, but it is much easier to buy attention than it is to earn credibility.

The brands I like most feel clear. You understand what they care about, how they sound, and why they’re showing up in the first place.

This matters even more in categories that ask people for trust. The work has to do more than look polished.

Spring 2026 / Creator strategy

Creator programs should feel more like communities and less like media buys.

The best creator partnerships rarely feel like one-off transactions. They feel like a brand found the right people to carry its voice into the world.

That changes the job. It becomes less about squeezing deliverables out of a brief and more about building something creators actually want to stay part of.

I’m interested in the difference between a campaign that technically worked and a program creators genuinely want to keep showing up for.

Spring 2026 / Technology

Technology brands need better briefs, not just louder campaigns.

When a category is new, fast-moving, or even a little intimidating, the brief matters more than people think. It shapes the tone, the clarity, and whether the final story sounds like a person or a brochure.

I’m especially interested in how technology brands show up in public. The temptation is often to be louder and more futuristic than necessary. Usually the better move is to be clearer.

It is just as much a communication problem as a product problem.
