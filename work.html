Global creator marketing

Lufthansa Group

Across Lufthansa, SWISS, and Austrian Airlines, I worked on creator and partnership campaigns built to make premium travel feel current, social, and worth talking about.

The work ranged from Pride and Fashion Week to destination storytelling, product launches, and year-round brand visibility. It required strong creator fit, sharp briefs, platform instincts, and constant judgment about what still felt right for the brand.

This is the kind of work I like most because it combines systems and taste. The process matters, the relationships matter, and the creative still has to hold up.

  • Topline: A 35-creator ecosystem producing 300+ assets, 8M+ organic views, and 30M+ paid impressions.
  • Program shape: Creator partnerships spanning product launches, destination storytelling, and cultural moments across multiple brands.
  • Platforms: Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube.
  • High-performing moments: Pride, Fashion Week, destination storytelling, ski races, jazz festival, and premium cabin launches.

Purpose-led branded storytelling

Brand New World Studios

Before creator strategy became a bigger part of my work, I spent years learning how to tell brand stories without sanding off the humanity.

At Brand New World Studios in Atlanta, I produced films about Oprah Winfrey, Little Miss Flint, Hershey, and the Salvation Army. Polished visuals weren't enough. The work needed a point of view.

That experience still shapes how I think as a marketer. A campaign can be organized, strategic, and well-targeted, but if it feels lifeless, people know.

  • Projects: Purpose-led branded films and documentaries across social, editorial, and longer-form formats.
  • Contribution: Creative development, production, narrative framing, and message discipline.
  • Takeaway: Story craft is a brand skill, not just a film skill.

Original work and post-production

Independent film, documentary, and VFX

Most of my creative instincts were built here. The marketing brain came later.

I’ve written, directed, and produced original short films including The Happiness Machine and A Trip to Remember, co-wrote and co-directed the documentary A New Coke, and worked in VFX supervision and editing on projects including Baba I’m Fine, an SXSW Official Selection in 2025.

Those projects taught me how to solve creative problems quickly, keep people aligned around a story, and work through constraints without panicking.

  • Original films: Writing, directing, producing, editing, and remote collaboration.
  • VFX: Supervision, editorial precision, and post-production problem solving.
  • Awards: Kiki McCabe and Agnes Nixon Screenwriting Award at Emory University.
