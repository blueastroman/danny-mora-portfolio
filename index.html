I build brands through story, partnerships, and cultural relevance.

I’m a New York-based marketer and filmmaker, born in the United States and raised in San José, Costa Rica. My work spans global airline marketing, creator programs, and narrative-led production for brands and original films.

How I think

The principles behind the work.

I care about performance, but the work usually gets stronger when the underlying thinking gets stronger first: the brief, the relationship, the tone, the moment, and the reason someone would genuinely care.

Story before format

Platforms change quickly. A good story still gives the work its staying power.

Relationships over transactions

Creator work is strongest when the partnership feels long-term, clear, and mutually aligned.

Premium needs precision

Luxury or high-trust brands cannot afford vague messaging or mediocre creative.

Technology still needs humanity

The more complicated the category, the more the work has to feel clear, useful, and emotionally legible.

Now

What I’m focused on right now.

I keep returning to the same question: how do brands show up intelligently when attention is fractured, audiences want more authenticity, and emerging tools keep changing the rules of communication?

Lately I’ve been especially interested in creator communities, briefing quality, global cultural moments, and the kinds of brand systems that make ambitious work easier to repeat instead of harder to sustain.

I’m also paying close attention to how AI is changing expectations around speed, usefulness, taste, and trust, and what that means for marketers trying to stay human.
